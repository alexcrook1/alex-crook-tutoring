About me

A qualified teacher, not just a tutor.

Ten years in the classroom — including senior leadership — means I know what good science teaching actually looks like.

I'm a qualified science teacher with 10 years of experience across secondary and sixth form, specialising in Physics up to and including A-Level. I've held senior roles including Head of Science and Head of Year, and I'm currently teaching internationally.

Tutoring appeals to me because of the one-to-one dynamic. When it's just the two of us, I can actually listen to how a student thinks — find where the gaps are, and build from there at their pace. No class of 30. No one-size-fits-all.

My focus is KS4 and exam preparation. If your student is approaching their GCSEs and needs someone to help them consolidate, close the gaps, and walk into the exam hall with real confidence — that's exactly what I do.

My approach

Tutoring that adapts to the student.

Every session is shaped by where the student is in their journey — not a fixed script.

01
Conversational early on

With KS3 students or those earlier in KS4, I keep things relaxed. The goal is to make them feel comfortable asking questions, thinking out loud, and making mistakes without embarrassment.

02
Focused as exams approach

As the exam window gets closer, sessions shift: past papers, command word technique, mark scheme logic, and learning to work efficiently under timed conditions.

03
Targeted gap-closing

I don't waste time on what a student already knows. I find the gaps quickly and close them systematically — using the AQA specification as the map.

How it works

What to expect from a session.

Sessions are structured, purposeful and run for one hour over Google Meet.

0–15 min
Instruction & explanation

We start with the topic — clear explanation, worked examples, and checking understanding before moving on.

15–45 min
Guided tasks & practice

Students work through questions and problems with me on hand to guide, prompt and correct in real time.

45–60 min
Questions & review

Time set aside for the student to ask anything, review what we've covered, and agree on what to practise before next time.

Sessions take place over Google Meet and run for one hour. Everything you need — resources, tasks and meeting links — is available through your student portal before each session.

I share my screen, we work through problems together on shared documents, and students can ask questions at any point. It's designed to feel as close to sitting beside a teacher as an online session can.

Independent study

There will be an expectation for students to complete some tasks between sessions. This isn't busywork — it's targeted practice based on what we've covered, and it's what makes the sessions actually stick.
